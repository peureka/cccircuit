Privacy

Last updated: 29 April 2026

This policy explains what personal data the Circuit membership collects, why we collect it, and your rights over it. It applies to your use of circuit.fm, your Circuit membership, and the events you attend through us. For the contract that governs membership generally, see our Terms.

1. Who we are

The Circuit membership at circuit.fm is operated by Avdience Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales. Avdience Ltd is the data controller for personal data processed in connection with your Circuit membership. You can contact us about anything in this policy at circuit@avdience.com.

The membership runs on technical infrastructure provided by Circuit Limited (a separate company that operates the venue and operator platform at meetcircuit.com). Circuit Limited acts as our data processor for the membership system under a Data Processing Agreement.

2. What we collect

We try to collect as little as possible. The data we hold falls into four categories:

Identity. Email address (required at signup). Name (optional). Profile fields you choose to fill in via the profile form: Instagram handle, neighbourhoods, available nights, format preferences. We do not collect your phone number, date of birth, address, or any payment data.

Presence. When you tap a Block at a venue, we record that you tapped, which Block, and the timestamp. This is how attendance works. We do not use GPS, Bluetooth beacons, Wi-Fi triangulation, or any form of ambient location tracking. Tap equals consent. No tap, no data.

Communications. We send transactional emails (signup confirmation, profile-completion reminders) and broadcast emails (curated outings to your segment). We log whether each email was delivered, opened, and which links were clicked — see Section 5 for the detail.

Site usage. When you visit circuit.fm we record your IP address, user-agent, and the pages you viewed via Vercel's analytics. We do not use cookies for advertising, retargeting, or cross-site tracking.

3. Why we use it

We process your data for these purposes only:

  • To operate your membership: keep you on the list, send you outings you'd actually go to, recognise you at the door.
  • To run the presence layer at venues so other members can see you're there if you've chosen to be visible.
  • To send transactional and broadcast emails relevant to your membership.
  • To measure aggregate engagement so we can improve the programme (which formats land, which neighbourhoods underserve members).
  • To meet our legal obligations — respond to data subject requests, comply with court orders, prevent fraud.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling for advertising, or training machine learning models. We do not sell your data.

4. Legal basis (UK GDPR)

Under Article 6 of the UK GDPR, we rely on:

  • Consent when you sign up to receive emails from us. You can withdraw at any time via the unsubscribe link in any email or by emailing circuit@avdience.com.
  • Contract for everything necessary to provide membership: presence at venues, RSVPs, recognition at the door, your member profile.
  • Legitimate interests for aggregate site analytics, fraud prevention, and securing the service. We've assessed these against your privacy interests and consider the impact minimal.

5. Email tracking

Broadcast emails sent through Circuit (curated outings, member updates) include open and click tracking via our email provider (Resend). This tells us whether the email landed and which links you clicked. We use this to understand which outings actually resonate, not to build a profile of you.

Every email includes a one-click unsubscribe link. Using it removes you from all future Circuit marketing emails immediately. Transactional emails (signup confirmation, security notices) cannot be opted out of while you're a member — if you don't want them, leave the membership entirely.

6. Sharing your data

We share data only with service providers that operate parts of the Circuit infrastructure on our behalf. They process your data as our data processors, under written agreements that bind them to use it only for our purposes:

  • Circuit Limited (operator platform at meetcircuit.com) — hosts the membership database, presence ledger, and event/RSVP system. Based in the UK; the underlying database (Neon) is in the EU.
  • Resend — sends transactional and broadcast emails. Based in the United States.
  • Firebase / Google Cloud — hosts the circuit.fm signup queue (Firestore). Data stored in EU multi-region.
  • Vercel — hosts circuit.fm itself and provides anonymised page-view analytics. Edge functions run globally; data origin is the United States.

We do not share your data with venues, organisers, or third-party advertisers. Venues see only aggregate attendance counts for their own events. Organisers see members who RSVP to their event but not your wider membership profile.

7. International data transfers

Some of our processors (Resend, Vercel) are based in the United States. When data leaves the UK or EU we rely on the UK Information Commissioner's International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), as appropriate. If you'd like the specific transfer mechanism for any processor, email us and we'll send it.

8. Cookies and tracking

circuit.fm uses one cookie set by Vercel for anonymised analytics (visitor counts, page views, referrer). It does not contain your email or any identifier that links to your member account.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies, retargeting pixels, or cross-site trackers. We do not run analytics scripts beyond Vercel's first-party one.

9. Data retention

How long we keep things, in plain terms:

  • Email and profile — for as long as you are a member, plus 12 months after membership ends, in case you re-join.
  • Tap / attendance ledger — retained for the lifetime of your membership. Aggregated venue-level statistics may be retained beyond that in non-personal form.
  • Email delivery logs (open/click events) — 12 months from the date of send.
  • Site analytics — 30 days, then aggregated and anonymised.
  • Backup snapshots — deleted on a 35-day rolling window.

If you exercise your right to erasure, we anonymise your record rather than deleting it — your name and email are removed, but your historical attendance counts remain in aggregated form. This protects the integrity of the venue's records and our analytics; it cannot be used to re-identify you.

10. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify data that is wrong or out of date. Most fields you can edit yourself via your profile page.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data. We honour this except where we have a legal obligation to keep records (none currently apply to standard membership data).
  • Restrict processing in certain circumstances.
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where consent is the basis for processing.

To exercise any of these rights, email circuit@avdience.com. We respond within one calendar month and typically much faster.

You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk if you believe we've mishandled your data.

11. Children

Circuit is for adults. Our events run at venues with their own age policies, typically 18+. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 16. If you believe a minor has signed up, email us and we will delete the record.

12. Security

We use industry-standard security: encryption in transit (TLS), encryption at rest, access controls scoped to need, and short retention windows. No system is perfectly secure. If we ever suffer a breach affecting your data, we will notify you and the ICO within the timeframes UK GDPR requires.

13. Changes

We may update this policy. We will notify members of material changes via email at least 30 days before they take effect. Continued use of Circuit after that notice period constitutes acceptance. If you do not agree, you may cancel membership at any time by emailing circuit@avdience.com.
